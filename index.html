📊 A Note on Education & History

According to the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP — "The Nation's Report Card"), Southern states consistently rank among the lowest in the country in 8th-grade reading and math scores. States like Mississippi, Louisiana, Alabama, and Arkansas regularly finish in the bottom five nationally.

Massachusetts, by contrast, is the #1 ranked state in the nation for K–12 academic achievement. The gap is not coincidental — it correlates strongly with historical underinvestment in public education, particularly in states that once denied education to enslaved people by law and resisted federal desegregation orders for decades after the Civil War.

History has consequences. Education is how we change them.
